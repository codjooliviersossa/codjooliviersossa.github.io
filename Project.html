Project 01: Highest-order asymptotic theory in the class of heteroscedastic symmetric nonlinear models

Authors: Sossa C.O and Audrey H. M.A.C

High-order asymptotic theory in regression models provides an advanced understanding of the behavior of estimators in these complex models. However, previous studies have focused predominantly on linear dispersion modeling, leaving non-linear dispersion modeling, leaving non-linear dispersion modeling less documented. In this context, the present project aims to fill this gap, focusing specifically on nonlinear symmetric heteroscedastic (MNLSH) models. Thus, we will extend the class of MNLSH models by allowing the mean and dispersion link functions to be nonlinear functions that depend on a set of unknown parameters to be estimated, with multiplicative heteroscedasticity as a particular case. Two lines of research in asymptotic theory will be addressed in this work. The first deals with reducing the bias of maximum likelihood estimators (MLEs) in the class of MNLSH models via Firth's methodology (1993), making it possible to obtain corrected estimators which, in principle, are more accurate than uncorrected estimators. Bootstrap-corrected biased estimators will also be considered. In addition, we present different types of confidence intervals. The second line of research addresses the derivation of adjustments to the likelihood ratio test statistic proposed by Skovgaard (2001) with the aim of improving the quality of inferences about the mean and dispersion regression parameters in MNLSH models. The performance of the estimators and hypothesis tests will be evaluated numerically and compared to their uncorrected versions through Monte Carlo simulation studies, in terms of size and power, on finite samples. Finally, real data sets will be used to evaluate our theoretical results.

Project 02: Indigenous women social promoters of participatory diagnosis: In defense of ancestral knowledge and memory of water, indigenous women for the protection of cultural and intangible heritage in Pankararu Territory.

Authors:Silva, E.C and Sossa C.O

The project's main objective is to analyze the relationship between indigenous women and access to water in the Pankararu Territory, in the hinterland of Pernambuco, based on the development of socio-educational studies and actions aimed at strengthening the role of indigenous Pankararu women to act as educators and social promoters in the construction of a participatory ethnodiagnosis of the conditions of access to water for indigenous families in the Pankararu territory.

Project 03: Ethnomapping and ethnodiagnosis of the living and working conditions of Pankararu indigenous families living in the urban and rural areas of the municipality of Petrolândia, PE..

Authors: Silva, E.C and Sossa C.O

The project aims to carry out a social and participatory ethnodiagnosis of the living and working conditions of indigenous families living in the rural and urban areas of the municipality of Petrolândia.
